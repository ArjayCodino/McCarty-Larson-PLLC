What is adoption in Texas?

Adoption is the legal process by which a person or a couple becomes the legal parent(s) of a child who is not their biological child. In Texas, adoption is governed by the Texas Family Code. There are different types of adoptions, including domestic adoptions, international adoptions, and stepparent adoptions. Each type of adoption has its own set of legal requirements and procedures.

To adopt a child in Texas, prospective adoptive parents must meet certain eligibility requirements, including being at least 21 years old, financially stable, and able to provide a safe and loving home for the child. They must also undergo a criminal background check, a child abuse and neglect check, and a home study. Once these requirements are met, the adoption process can begin.
The process typically involves working with an adoption agency or an adoption attorney, attending adoption training classes, and completing various legal documents. The length of the adoption process can vary depending on the type of adoption, the age of the child, and other factors. However, the ultimate goal of adoption is to provide a child with a permanent and loving home.

Types of Adoption

Adoption is a beautiful and life-changing process that can bring joy and fulfillment to both adoptive parents and children. In Texas, there are several types of adoption available to those who wish to expand their family. The different types of adoption in Texas and what you need to know about each of them.

  • Domestic Infant Adoption
    Domestic infant adoption is the most common type of adoption in Texas. It involves adopting a newborn baby from within the United States, usually through a private adoption agency or an attorney. Birth parents typically choose the adoptive family and often have ongoing contact with the child after placement. Prospective adoptive parents must meet certain eligibility requirements and complete a home study to ensure they are suitable to become adoptive parents.

  • Foster Care Adoption
    Foster care adoption involves adopting a child who is in the care of the state due to abuse, neglect, or other reasons that prevent them from living with their birth family. Adoptive parents can be matched with a child through the Texas Department of Family and Protective Services (DFPS) or a licensed foster care agency. The child's birth parents may have limited involvement or no involvement in the adoption process. Prospective adoptive parents must complete a home study and meet certain eligibility requirements.

  • International Adoption
    International adoption involves adopting a child from another country. Prospective adoptive parents must work with a Hague-accredited adoption agency to complete the adoption process. The process can be complex and may take longer than domestic adoption. Prospective adoptive parents must also meet certain eligibility requirements and complete a home study

  • Stepparent Adoption
    Stepparent adoption is a type of adoption in which a stepparent legally adopts their spouse's child. This type of adoption is typically less complicated than other types of adoption and may not require a home study or extensive background checks. However, the stepparent must still meet certain eligibility requirements and obtain consent from the child's other biological parent.

  • Relative Adoption
    Relative adoption involves a family member, such as a grandparent, aunt, or uncle, adopting a child who is related to them by blood or marriage. This type of adoption may be arranged through a private attorney or through the DFPS. Prospective adoptive parents must still meet certain eligibility requirements and complete a home study.

Adoption Process in Texas

The adoption process in Texas can be a complex and lengthy journey, but it is ultimately rewarding for both the adoptive parents and the child. The process involves a number of steps, including completing a home study, obtaining necessary documentation, and attending pre-adoption education and training. Once these steps are completed, the adoptive parents will be matched with a child or children who meet their preferences and criteria.

One unique aspect of the adoption process in Texas is the emphasis on preserving the child's cultural and religious heritage. Adoptive parents are encouraged to consider adopting children from their own cultural and ethnic backgrounds, and they may need to demonstrate their ability to provide a home that is respectful of the child's heritage.

Additionally, the state offers post-adoption resources and support services to ensure that adoptive families can successfully navigate the challenges of welcoming a new child into their home. Overall, the adoption process in Texas requires patience and dedication, but it can provide a loving home for a child in need.

Legal Considerations in Adoption


Adopting a child can be a fulfilling experience for prospective parents in Texas, but it is important to understand the legal considerations involved in the process. Firstly, Texas requires that all potential adoptive parents must undergo a criminal background check, including fingerprints. This is to ensure the safety of the child and to make sure that the prospective parents do not have a criminal history that would make them ineligible to adopt.

Another important legal consideration is the termination of the biological parents' rights. In Texas, a court may terminate a parent's rights if they have abandoned the child, committed abuse or neglect, or if they are unfit to parent. Once the biological parent's rights are terminated, the child becomes legally free for adoption. It is important to note that the process of terminating parental rights can be complex and may involve multiple court hearings.
